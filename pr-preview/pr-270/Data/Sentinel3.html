Sentinel-3

The main objective of the Copernicus Sentinel-3 mission is to measure ocean and land surface colour, sea and land surface temperature, and sea surface topography with high accuracy and reliability to support ocean forecasting systems, environmental monitoring and climate monitoring. The mission definition is driven by the need for continuity in provision of ERS, ENVISAT and SPOT vegetation data, with improvements in instrument performance and coverage.

Level-0 Level-1 Level-2

Sentinel-3 OLCI Level 1 EFR

Overview

Sentinel-3 OLCI Level 1 EFR (Earth Observation Full Resolution) observes and measures the color of oceans and land surfaces. The Level 1 EFR data includes calibrated and corrected measurements, accounting for factors like sensor issues and atmospheric effects. Scientists use this data to study ocean color, monitor coastal areas, analyze vegetation and land use changes, and understand Earth’s environment and climate.

Sentinel-3 OLCI Level 2 LFR

Overview

Sentinel-3 OLCI Level 2 LFR(Land Full Resolution) focuses on both land and ocean surfaces, providing information about ocean color, aquatic ecosystems, water quality, land cover changes, vegetation, and atmospheric conditions. The data undergoes atmospheric corrections, geometrical adjustments, and other processing steps to improve accuracy and usefulness. This dataset includes data on three primary variables: OLCI global vegetation index file; * terrestrial Chlorophyll index file; * integrated water vapor over water file.

Sentinel-3 OLCI Level 0

Overview

The Sentinel-3 OLCI Level 0 is the reconstructed and time-sorted Instrument Source Packet (ISP) at full space-time resolution. The first part of the process involves unpacking the ISPs, performing a quality check and appending annotation data to them. Once the input raw data files are read, all necessary data are extracted and parsed. The ISPs are then sorted and checked, including missing and duplicated packet numbering. The final part of the processing is the Level-0 product generation. Several quality flags are computed and included in the associated metadata. Raw data, time sorted and annotated are included in the Level-0 package.

Sentinel-3 SLSTR Level 2 AOD

Overview

Sentinel-3 SLSTR Level 2 AOD (Aerosol Optical Depth) provides information about the amount of aerosols present in the atmosphere, which affects sunlight absorption, scattering, and visibility. The data is obtained from radiance measurements and processed to quantify aerosol density across different wavelengths.

Sentinel-3 SLSTR Level 2 FRP

Overview

Sentinel-3 SLSTR Level 2 FRP (Fire Radiative Power) provides information about the thermal energy emitted by active fires on Earth’s surface. This data is obtained from radiance measurements and processed to estimate the intensity of the fire’s radiative power. SLSTR Level 2 FRP data is used to detect and monitor fires, assess their intensity, understand fire behavior, and aid in wildfire management and environmental monitoring efforts.

Sentinel-3 SLSTR Level 0

Overview

The Sentinel-3 SLSTR Level 0 product is the raw unprocessed data acquired by the SLSTR instrument on board the Sentinel-3 satellite. It consists of the uncalibrated digital counts received by the SLSTR detectors for each pixel in the image. It provides a valuable source of unprocessed data for researchers and advanced users who require access to the raw data for their applications.

Sentinel-3 SYN Level 2 Surface Directional Reflectance

Overview

The Level-2 SYN product (Surface Reflectance and Aerosol Parameters over Land) from the SYNERGY Level-2 processor comprises surface reflectance data for sun-reflective SLSTR channels (S1, S2, S3, S5, S6) in nadir and oblique views, as well as OLCI channels (excluding specific bands). This product is valuable for studying surface properties, aerosol characteristics, and environmental changes in Earth’s atmosphere and landscape.

Sentinel-3 SYN Level 2 VGP

Overview

The Sentinel-3 SYN Level 2 VGP (Vegetation like Product) is referenced as SPOT continuity product, adapted for scientific applications requiring highly accurate physical measurements. As a consequence, data are corrected for systematic errors (such as error registration of the different channels or calibration of all the detectors along the line-array detectors for each spectral band) and resampled to predefined geographic projections.
